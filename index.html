Importance of UX/UI Design

User Experience and User Interface Design can make the difference between whether your potential clients engage in your content or leave uninterested.

Ultimately, the purpose is to connect business goals with user's needs through research, development, testing, and refinement, bringing satisfaction to both sides of the relationship.

  • (Source)

    • UX Design

    “User experience encompasses all aspects of the end-user’s interaction with the company, its services, and its products.” – Don Norman, Cognitive Scientist & User Experience Architect.

    The "user experience" refers to the interaction between user and product services- the functionality of it, ease of use, and the adaptability for the user. The "design" refers to all of the elements that create that experience.

  • (Source)

    • UI Design

    User Interace Design refers to the look, feel, and interactivity of the product services.

    It encompasses all the visual, interactive elements of a product interface— including display screens, keyboards, mouse buttons, icons, spacing, typography, color schemes, responsive design, and the appearance of a desktop. It is basically the “skin” of the design.

  • (Source)

    • Bad UX/UI Designs

    Bad UX/UI can send your potential customers running for the hills, never to look back.

    Long page loading times and too many features stacked on top of one another are examples bad UX/UI designs that turn customers away quickly.

  • (Source2)

  • (Source1)

    • Good UX/UI Designs

    “Good design is actually a lot harder to notice than poor design, in part because good designs fit our needs so well that the design is invisible.”- Don Norman

    Good UX design will usually include things like research, wireframing, prototyping, testing, iteration cycles, and development planning.
